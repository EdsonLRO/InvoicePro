# Tallyo Business-User Data Processing Terms

These terms would supplement the final Tallyo Terms between `[CONTRACTING SOLE-TRADER NAME - OWNER TO SUPPLY SECURELY]`, trading as Tallyo (`Tallyo`), and the business user (`Customer`). They apply only to processing for which the Customer is confirmed as controller and Tallyo as processor. Tallyo remains controller for activities where it determines its own purposes and means, as explained in the final Privacy Notice.

## Processing Details

| Item | Working description |
|---|---|
| Subject matter | Hosting and operating the Customer's business profile, contacts, invoices, quotes, credit notes, saved items, transactional document/reminder email, payment-link/status features, export, support and deletion functions |
| Duration | The account term plus the approved closure, deletion, backup-expiry, dispute and legal-hold periods |
| Nature | Collection from the Customer; storage, organisation, retrieval, display, transmission, email delivery, payment-status reconciliation, export, correction, restriction and deletion on documented instruction |
| Purpose | Provide the Customer-selected Tallyo business-invoicing functions |
| People | The Customer's personnel and representatives; its customers, invoice recipients, suppliers or other business contacts; other people whose information the Customer lawfully includes |
| Data | Identity/business/contact details; document descriptions, dates and amounts; email/PDF content and delivery events; payment/refund/dispute summaries and provider identifiers; Customer-selected free text |
| Intended exclusions | Special-category, criminal-offence, medical, safeguarding, legal-case and similarly sensitive workflow data; children as target users/data subjects |

The final schedule must match the exact production product and provider configuration.

## Customer Instructions and Responsibilities

Tallyo will process covered data only on documented instructions from the Customer, including these terms and in-product actions, unless UK law requires otherwise. In that event Tallyo will inform the Customer before processing unless the law prohibits notice.

The Customer determines whether its collection, document, reminder, payment, retention, disclosure, and rights-response instructions are lawful. It must provide required privacy information, minimise data, keep contact details accurate, avoid prohibited sensitive workflows, control account access, maintain its own statutory records, and notify Tallyo of an unlawful or mistaken instruction. Tallyo must inform the Customer if it believes an instruction infringes applicable data-protection law, subject to qualified wording.

## Confidentiality and Security

Tallyo will limit covered-data access to authorised persons bound by confidentiality and will maintain measures appropriate to the assessed risk. Current measures include Supabase authentication, optional TOTP MFA, one-time recovery credentials, session controls, RLS tenant isolation, server-side authorization, signed Stripe webhook verification, encryption provided by contracted services, backups, and minimised audit events.

The final security schedule must state implemented measures accurately, review them after material changes, and avoid any absolute security guarantee. Customer security responsibilities and incident-contact routes must also be specified.

## Subprocessors

The working subprocessor categories include Supabase for hosting/backend, Resend and its delivery chain for email, Stripe where it processes on Customer instructions, and the approved production host. Google Workspace would be relevant only if purchased for restricted case assistance. Cloudflare Turnstile would be relevant only if activated.

Before publication, provide the final subprocessor list, locations, functions, contract route, objection/change notice process, and consequences of a substantiated objection. Tallyo remains responsible for processor obligations delegated to a subprocessor to the extent required by applicable law and the final contract.

## International Transfers

Tallyo will not make a restricted transfer of covered data without an applicable UK transfer mechanism and required safeguards. The exact adequacy, UK IDTA/Addendum, onward-transfer, location, risk-assessment, and supplementary-measure position for every provider must be approved before launch. On request, Tallyo will provide the information required by the final contract without exposing other customers' confidential or security information.

## Assistance

Taking account of the nature of processing and information available, Tallyo will provide the contractually defined reasonable assistance for:

- data-subject rights, including access, correction, deletion, restriction, objection and portability where applicable;
- security and personal-data breach investigation, mitigation, documentation and notification;
- DPIAs and any required prior consultation; and
- demonstrating compliance with the Customer's applicable controller obligations.

The Customer remains responsible for deciding and communicating its controller response. The final terms must define intake, identity/authority checks, secure delivery, timeframes, scope, costs for exceptional requests, and any lawful refusal.

## Personal-Data Breaches

Tallyo will notify the Customer without undue delay after becoming aware of a personal-data breach affecting covered data and provide available information needed for the Customer's assessment. The final clause must define approved contacts, phased updates, cooperation, evidence preservation, no premature admission, and allocation of regulator/person notifications. This does not promise that every security incident is a reportable personal-data breach.

## Return, Export and Deletion

The Customer can use the approved export capability during account operation and any approved closure window. On termination or documented instruction, Tallyo will return or delete covered data according to the final schedule unless applicable law requires retention. The working proposal is a 30-day export/closure window, deletion from active workspace systems, and normal seven-day managed-backup expiry, with restore reconciliation.

The final clause must describe Auth, provider, email, payment, audit, legal-hold, subprocessor and backup limitations. It must not promise immediate or complete provider deletion where Tallyo cannot deliver it.

## Information and Audit

Tallyo will make available information reasonably necessary to demonstrate the processor obligations confirmed in the final terms and will support proportionate audits or inspections. The final process must protect other customers, security, confidentiality, availability, and provider restrictions; prioritise current independent evidence and remote review; define notice and frequency; and preserve regulator powers and non-excludable rights.

## Priority and Changes

The final terms must state effective date, governing agreement, order of precedence, change notice, termination remedy, liability allocation, governing law, and signatures/acceptance method. No placeholder or working role statement becomes binding until qualified review and separate Owner approval.
