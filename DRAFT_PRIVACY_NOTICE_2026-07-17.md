Tallyo is operated by `[CONTRACTING SOLE-TRADER NAME - OWNER TO SUPPLY SECURELY]`, trading as Tallyo. Our contact address is `[APPROVED SERVICE/CONTACT ADDRESS - QUALIFIED REVIEW REQUIRED]` and our business, privacy, and data-protection complaint contact is `[APPROVED PUBLIC CONTACT ROUTE]`.

Where a business user decides why and how to use customer/contact and invoice information, that business user is expected to be the controller and Tallyo may process the information on its instructions. This role split is provisional and must be confirmed before publication.

## Initial Service Scope

Tallyo is intended initially for UK business account holders aged 18 or over using it for ordinary business invoicing. It is not intended for personal or household account use, users under 18, or workflows designed for special-category, criminal-offence, medical, safeguarding, legal-case, or similarly sensitive records.

## Information We Use

Depending on how a person interacts with Tallyo, the service may use:

- account information, such as email address, account identifiers, confirmation and essential settings;
- authentication and security information, such as MFA status, session and recovery state, security events, device/browser and network signals where necessary, and minimised audit records;
- business-profile and contact information entered by an account holder;
- customer, invoice, quote, credit-note and saved-item information, including names, business/contact details, descriptions, amounts and dates;
- document-delivery information, including recipient addresses, requested message/PDF content and delivery events;
- payment, refund and dispute summaries and provider identifiers; card details are entered on Stripe-hosted pages and are not intended to be stored by Tallyo;
- support, privacy-request, complaint and incident correspondence and decision records; and
- ordinary public-site request information processed by the hosting provider.

We may receive information directly from an account holder, from a business user who enters or sends a document to another person, from service providers, or from the person's use of Tallyo. Invoice recipients may therefore not have supplied their information directly to Tallyo.

## Retention

Tallyo keeps information only for a defined purpose and must implement category-specific deletion. The current proposed schedule is not final:

- active account identity/settings: while the account is active, then through the approved closure process;
- user workspace records: while active, followed by a proposed 30-day export/closure window and normal seven-day managed-backup expiry;
- Resend-held email content/metadata: the provider currently states 30 days for standard plans;
- routine security/operational events: proposed 12 months, with narrowly defined material-security evidence proposed for 24 months;
- routine support cases: proposed 24 months after closure;
- privacy requests and formal complaints: proposed three years after closure;
- confirmed personal-data breach or material legal-dispute records: proposed six years after final closure; and
- minimal Tallyo payment/refund/dispute evidence: proposed six years where necessary for approved tax, accounting or legal-claim purposes.

Exceptions may apply for a documented legal obligation, dispute, fraud/security investigation, court/regulatory requirement, legal hold, provider limitation, or backup expiry. Business users are responsible for exporting and retaining their own accounting records. Final periods, start events, fields and deletion methods require qualified approval and technical acceptance before publication.

## Your Rights and Complaints

Depending on the circumstances, a person may have rights to be informed, access, correction, deletion, restriction, portability, objection, or withdrawal of consent where consent is actually used. Rights can be limited by the applicable role, law, exemptions, other people's rights, and Tallyo's processor obligations.

Contact `[APPROVED PUBLIC CONTACT ROUTE]` with a request or data-protection complaint. We may ask for proportionate information to verify identity and authority, but do not send identity documents unless specifically requested through an approved secure method. If a business user controls the relevant invoice/contact information, we may route or assist the request with that business user.

Tallyo must provide a clear complaint route, acknowledge a data-protection complaint within 30 days, investigate and keep the person informed without undue delay, and communicate the outcome without undue delay. A person may also complain to the Information Commissioner's Office at <https://ico.org.uk/make-a-complaint/>. The final wording must not imply that contacting Tallyo removes any right to approach the ICO.

## Security

Tallyo uses measures including account authentication, optional TOTP MFA, one-time recovery codes, session controls, tenant isolation through row-level security, server-side authorization, signed Stripe webhook verification, backups, and minimised audit events. No system or transmission can be guaranteed completely secure. Never send passwords, authenticator secrets, recovery codes, card details, or unnecessary sensitive information through support.
